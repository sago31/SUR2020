Higher Education is a structure where students are confined outside of their natural territories, each with their own needs, desires and struggles. This garden has to be taken care both collectively and individually. They may thrive in this environment or they may perish if they are not able to adapt. They all need to grow in order to blossom.

 

The exploration of my south has led me to discover new territories, mystical lands at the bottom of the globe that were often described as savage and unstable. I have found a tropical garden. The vegetation is so dense I can barely distinguish where one plant starts and the other ends, all I see is an intricate system of colours.

 

I walk closer to one of the colours in front of me, it is a plant that has been growing for a while, you can see it is supported by the whole system but is taking its own path. There is a story inside of it, you can see the past and the future unravelling like a blossoming flower. Their scent is the knowledge that has been created, it is calling other beings.

 

Although they appear to be a single being when you come closer and start to pay attention each plant has its own unique characteristics, strengths and weaknesses.

They might be some that are asking for more water or light, or some that may have found a balance and whilst they appear to be still, they are patiently growing.

For the plant to live in the garden it needs to adapt to a strange place quite dissimilar to its place of origin.

 

This intricate system relies on solidarity, when you enter you have to take care of the garden, you are responsible for the other and you have to make sure you are also growing. You have to provide the nutrients, the water and the soil for each plant to be able to achieve its potential.

 

Every plant grows differently, all of them can choose a different path, downwards and upwards, backwards or forward or all of them simultaneously. Down below in the ground, the garden spreads, all plants are connected in one way or another.
